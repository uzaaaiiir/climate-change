Background

Climate Change: What is it?

Climate change is when the average global weather conditions change. Over the past decades, human activities have produced enormouse amounts of greenhouse gases. These gases get trapped in our atmosphere. As a result, global temperatures increase and harm our planet. We see numerous effects as a result of global warming.

Poor air quality

Longer periods of drought

Increased flooding and rising oceans

Frequent cold and heat waves

Stronger storms, cyclones, and hurricanes

Species extinction and dying ecosystems

Causes

Modern Industrial Society is Inherently Destructive

Energy

Contributes to 30% of global emissions! Coal is the most significant contributor.

Transportation

Transportant contributes to over 20% of global carbon emissions. Land-based travel is a significant threat to the environment.

Agriculture

Agriculture contributes 13-18% of global emissions! We rely on agriculture for food and the demand for food is steadily increasing.

Fashion

The fashion industry currently produces 10% of the global carbon emissions! By 2050, it could produce up to a quarter of emissions.
