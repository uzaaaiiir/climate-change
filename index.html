Background

Climate Change: What is it?

Climate change is when the average global weather conditions change. Over the past decades, human activities have produced enormouse amounts of greenhouse gases. These gases get trapped in our atmosphere. As a result, global temperatures increase and harm our planet. We see numerous effects as a result of global warming.

Poor air quality

Longer periods of drought

Increased flooding and rising oceans

Frequent cold and heat waves

Stronger storms, cyclones, and hurricanes

Species extinction and dying ecosystems

Causes

Modern Industrial Society is Inherently Destructive

Energy

Contributes to 30% of global emissions! Coal is the most significant contributor.

Transportation

Transportant contributes to over 20% of global carbon emissions. Land-based travel is a significant threat to the environment.

Agriculture

Agriculture contributes 13-18% of global emissions! We rely on agriculture for food and the demand for food is steadily increasing.

Fashion

The fashion industry currently produces 10% of the global carbon emissions! By 2050, it could produce up to a quarter of emissions.

Grey Areas

It's More Complex Than You Think

Poverty vs Prosperity

Richer people contribute to more emissions. But, 63% of globale emissions come from low to middle income countries.

Global emissions are produced from people trying to escape poverty and get to a comfortable lifestyle. This create unavoidable emissions produced to try and meet basic needs.

Agriculture and Food Production

We have a growing population, and we can't feed this population wthout greenhouse gases.

Modern food production involves unavoidable emission of greenhouse gases. It's impossible to have zero-emission food.

57% of food emissions come from animal-based food and they only make up 18% of global calories supply.

Climate Change is a Systemic Reality

The systemic reality of global warming is bigger than one person can solve. Taking personal responsibility by using metal straws will not solve climate change.

If you removed 100% of your emissions for the rest of your life, you would ONLY save one second worth of emissions from the global energy sector.
